EQUITY FRAMEWORK

Chicago Public Schools

In collaboration with Convergence Design Lab Chicago and the Chicago Public Schools Office of Equity. I was the lead Equity Designer for the CPS Equity Framework -- responsible for visualizing academic journals into interactive print & web collateral.

So how'd that happen?

Needfinding

To best inform the redesign of the academic writing, needfinding research was done to understand, client, audience, and user needs.

Needfinding

Our client needs:

1. A Style Guide to create a uniform design
2. Visualization of their Academic Writing
3. Cross-Platform Design (designs that would work in a PDF, on a powerpoint, and on a website)

Needfinding

Our Audience

1. Who are they? Parents and Community Leaders
2. as a parent, how does equity affect my child(ren), how can my child thrive in an equity based environment?
3. how can we, the community leaders, use equity to empower youth beyond the classroom?

Needfinding

Our Users

1. Who are they? Educators & Administration in CPS Schools
2. how can I, the educator, implement Equity in the classroom?
3. how can we, the administration, empower teachers to use Equity based practices in the classroom and beyond?

Needfinding

Stakeholder Common Goals

1. Implementing Equity Practices in the classroom
2. Equity Awareness

Brainstorming

Based on our user research, we wanted to see the overlap between our stakeholder needs. All of these groups had common goals of equity awareness and implementing equity practices in the classroom. This influenced our ideation. We kept in mind our stakeholder needs as to design the best possible tool for them.
